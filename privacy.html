Why This Matters

Your data is someone else's product.

Every file you store in the cloud, every email you send, every password you save — it's sitting on a server you don't own, managed by a company with different interests than yours.

🤖 AI trains on your documents

Microsoft, Google, and others use uploaded files to train AI models. Your client contracts, financial records, and case files may be feeding systems you never agreed to.

🔓 Cloud breaches are routine

Every major cloud provider has experienced breaches. When your data is centralized with millions of others, you're a target by association — not because of anything you did.

📋 Professional confidentiality at risk

Lawyers, accountants, and consultants have legal and ethical obligations to protect client information. Storing that data on third-party cloud servers is a gray area at best.

💸 You're paying to lose control

Microsoft 365 costs $150–300/yr per user. Google Workspace similar. You pay monthly — then they change terms, raise prices, or get breached. You have no leverage.

Common Questions

Answered plainly.

Do I need to be technically minded to use any of this?
No. The day-to-day experience — sharing a file, saving a password, chatting with a teammate — looks like the apps you already use. The complexity is entirely on our side. We set it up, keep it running, and explain what each thing does in plain English.
What happens if the mini-PC dies?
We source a replacement — same model, same configuration — typically within a day or two. Because we standardize on known hardware, we can often restore your setup in under an hour from the encrypted backup. Your data is never at single-point risk.
Can I still use Google or Microsoft tools if I want?
Absolutely. Local First is an option, not a mandate. We're not asking you to throw away your existing tools. Most clients use a mix — local server for sensitive client files and passwords, Google for general internal stuff. We help you figure out where the lines make sense for your practice.
Do I need this if I already have IT support elsewhere?
Most traditional IT providers push cloud-first setups because they're easier to manage remotely. Very few offer a local-first alternative. If data ownership matters to your practice, this is unlikely to be something your current provider is offering — or even recommending.
